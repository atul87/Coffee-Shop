Brewing Tips & Techniques

Perfect Espresso Extraction

+

For the perfect espresso, use water heated to 195-205°F (90-96°C). Grind your beans to a fine consistency, similar to table salt. Tamp the grounds evenly with 30 pounds of pressure. The extraction should take 25-30 seconds for a double shot, producing about 2 ounces of liquid.

  • Use a 1:2 ratio (18g of coffee to 36g of water)
  • Preheat your portafilter and cup
  • Grind just before brewing for maximum freshness

French Press Brewing Method

+

The French press method produces a rich, full-bodied cup of coffee. Use a coarse grind, about 1:15 ratio (30g coffee to 450ml water). Heat water to just off boiling, add coffee, stir, and steep for 4 minutes before pressing.

  • Use a timer to ensure consistent steeping
  • Stir the crust that forms after 1 minute
  • Press slowly and steadily to avoid over-extraction

Pour Over Coffee Technique

+

Pour over coffee allows for precise control over brewing variables. Use a medium-fine grind and a 1:16 ratio. Start by rinsing the filter, then add coffee and pour water in a circular motion. The total brew time should be 3-4 minutes.

  • Bloom the coffee by pouring twice its weight in water first
  • Maintain a slow, steady pour
  • Adjust grind size to control extraction time
